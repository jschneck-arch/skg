# SKG Folds And Points Of Interest

Purpose: define the structures that matter most during an SKG engagement and why they matter operationally.

This document is not about findings in the usual security sense.
It is about pressure in the observational field.

## Why Folds Matter

Folds are one of the main outputs of SKG.

If projections show what the current model can express, folds show where the model, instrument set, or observation path is insufficient.

That is why folds are central to real strengthening:

- they expose missing structure
- they expose where observation is weak
- they expose where the catalog is underspecified
- they expose where a system would otherwise drift into assumption

## Fold Types

### Structural

Meaning:
- a service or surface is observed but SKG lacks toolchain/domain coverage

Operational response:
- toolchain generation
- new observation routes
- domain expansion

### Contextual

Meaning:
- a specific observed condition exists but current wickets do not express it

Examples:
- unmapped CVEs
- config/version/detail that the model cannot currently collapse

Operational response:
- catalog growth
- new wickets
- better mapping between observation and substrate structure

### Projection

Meaning:
- the observed surface implies an attack path or causal chain that is not yet modeled

Operational response:
- attack-path growth
- cross-domain relation growth
- new causal structure in the catalog

### Temporal

Meaning:
- prior evidence has decayed past its trusted lifetime

Operational response:
- re-observation
- confirmation
- explicit refusal to treat stale evidence as current state

## Points Of Interest Beyond Folds

Folds are not the only field structures that matter.

Other important points of interest include:

- high-energy regions
  - many unresolved conditions
- repeated blocked paths
  - stable obstruction that shapes feasible action
- persistent indeterminacy
  - uncertainty that remains after multiple instruments
- pearl clusters
  - repeated collapse structure over time
- growth-memory pressure
  - repeated model insufficiency around one identity/domain/family
- cross-domain consequence chains
  - foothold in one domain producing measured effect in another
- decayed evidence
  - previously measured state that must not be treated as persistent without re-check

These should be surfaced to the operator and assistant as first-class engagement structures.

## What Makes A Real Test

If SKG only confirms what the current model already encodes, it is performing re-check, not discovery.

A real test includes:

- conditions not fully explained by the current model
- folds that force model or observation growth
- cases where SKG remains correctly indeterminate
- cases where SKG must distinguish target-side blockage from instrument-side limitation
- repeated cause/effect traces over time

## Operator Role

The operator should be able to ask:

- why is this a fold?
- what kind of fold is it?
- what evidence created it?
- what structure is missing?
- what is the least-cost next action?
- is this target-side, instrument-side, or model-side?

## AI Assistant Role

The AI assistant should be able to:

- explain folds and points of interest
- cluster related folds
- draft growth actions
- connect folds to prior pearl memory
- summarize why gravity is pulling toward a region

The assistant must not:

- invent resolution
- erase unresolved structure
- upgrade unsupported inference into state

## Immediate Engineering Meaning

SKG should continue to improve in this order:

1. observe folds reliably
2. preserve them
3. cluster them structurally
4. relate them to memory
5. route attention through them
6. only later, attempt bounded self-correction
